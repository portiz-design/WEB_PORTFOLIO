NEW MUSEUM OF NATURAL
& INDIGENOUS HISTORY

Botanical Garden and Zoo of the City of Asunción

Architect: Pedro Ortiz
Project Category: Academic — Undergraduate Thesis
Institution: Universidad Americana
Media: AutoCAD, V-Ray, SketchUp
Height: 18.4 m
Floor Area: 3,231 m²
Structure: Reinforced concrete
Façade: Fibre cement cladding
Materials: Steel, stainless steel, glass, decorative concrete panels, onyx stone
Location: Botanical Garden and Zoo, Asunción

Background

Located within the Metropolitan Park of the Botanical Garden of Asunción, the project replaces the former museum housed in Casa Baja, a historic residence no longer suitable for the conservation and display of the institution’s collections.

The new building occupies a designated site within the protected park landscape and establishes a contemporary museum infrastructure integrated with its surroundings.

Concept

The museum is conceived as a horizontal architectural presence embedded within the landscape.

Its restrained scale and simple geometry avoid competing with the botanical environment and nearby historic structures. The architecture acts as a quiet framework where spatial clarity allows the artifacts and natural specimens to take precedence.

Structure and Materiality

The building is constructed with reinforced concrete and post-tensioned slabs, creating large and flexible exhibition spaces.

Exterior surfaces combine concrete panels and fiber-cement cladding selected for durability and low maintenance. Interior finishes prioritize clarity and robustness through porcelain flooring, drywall partitions, and stone accents.

Light and Circulation

Natural light becomes a central architectural element through carefully oriented glazed façades that illuminate the upper exhibition spaces while limiting direct solar exposure.

The museum experience follows a chronological spatial sequence. Visitors descend from the entrance to the underground level where geological and fossil collections are presented. Circulation gradually rises through exhibitions of fauna, flora, and indigenous artifacts, culminating in a rooftop garden displaying medicinal plants traditionally used in indigenous medicine.

Program

Underground Level
Administrative offices, theater, indigenous art gallery, geology and fossil exhibition hall, storage deposits, and conservation laboratory organized around an excavated open-air garden.

Ground Floor
Library with mezzanine herbarium archive, restaurant and terrace overlooking the garden, visitor services, souvenir shop, and a double-height exhibition gallery.

Upper Floor
Permanent exhibition rooms.

Rooftop Garden
An elevated botanical garden restoring part of the meadow landscape and reconnecting the museum experience with the surrounding environment.
