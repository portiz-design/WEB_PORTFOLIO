IMPRINT

Custom Cast Ring

Concept: An exploration of form, surface continuity, and memory expressed through wearable architecture.
Architect: Pedro Ortiz
Project Category: Academic — Graduate Design Studio
Institution: University of California, Davis
Media: Rhino, Illustrator, 3D-Printed Lost-Wax Casting
Location: California, USA
Structure: 14K Gold-Plated Copper
Materials: Copper, Gold, Wax

Background

IMPRINT investigates how architectural thinking can translate to object scale. The project explores mass, proportion, and surface continuity—principles commonly applied to buildings—through the design of a wearable artifact.

Developed digitally in Rhino and fabricated through a lost-wax casting process, the piece bridges computational modeling with traditional metal casting techniques, allowing precise control over geometry and surface articulation.

Design Strategy

The ring’s geometry draws inspiration from the compositional clarity of classical Roman architecture, emphasizing solidity, balance, and refined surfaces rather than ornamental complexity. The form is intentionally restrained: a continuous band whose proportions and polished finish convey permanence, durability, and tactile elegance.

Embedded Narrative

Within the minimal geometry, a subtle personal marker is integrated into the band: an embossed imprint derived from the nose of the client’s first dog, Copper. This detail transforms the object from a purely formal study into a personal artifact. The name “Copper” is engraved along the sides in a classical Roman typeface, reinforcing the historical references embedded within the design.

Material Logic

The ring is cast in copper and finished with a 14K gold plating. The copper core provides strength and warmth, while the gold surface introduces refinement and durability. This layered material approach emphasizes the relationship between structural core and finished surface, reinforcing the piece’s dual identity as both crafted object and personal heirloom.
