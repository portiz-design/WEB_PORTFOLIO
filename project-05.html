Poster 1 — The Politics of Materiality

Event Flyer

Background

This project was a graphic design course exercise in which I had to design a poster for a real event. The event I selected was the “Politics of Materiality” symposium at Parsons School of Design.

The event explores how architectural materiality is shaped by political, social, and cultural forces.

Design Concept

The poster blends visual collage with metaphor to explore the intersection of architecture and politics. A human figure ascends a set of brutalist concrete stairs, symbolizing how people navigate spaces shaped by material choices.

Above, a striking contrast between natural clouds and a sharp, artificial form underscores the event’s theme, highlighting the tension between the organic and the man-made.

This imagery challenges viewers to consider how political, social, and cultural ideologies influence the physical spaces we inhabit.

Poster 2 — Bracing for Impact

MFA Thesis Cover

Background

This poster was designed to serve as the cover for my MFA thesis project on climate change adaptation in architecture.

It visually communicates the relentless pursuit of innovation in architecture to address the ever-growing challenges of climate change, emphasizing safety, comfort, and hope even in seemingly dire situations.

Design Concept

Titled “Bracing for Impact,” the poster captures a poignant moment of upward contemplation and exploration beyond immediate boundaries.

This is visually represented by a human figure in a tunnel of white arches, looking up towards a starlit sky where an astronaut gazes back.

The design symbolizes the expansive role of architecture in adapting to environmental changes, underscoring the need for innovation in the face of the climate crisis.
