THE UNKNOWN, GALLERY

Exhibition Complex for Indigenous & Underrepresented Artists

Architect: Pedro Ortiz
Project Category: Academic, “Architectural Studio VIII” course
Design Completion Period & Year: 136 Hrs., 2017
Institution: Universidad Americana
Media: AutoCAD, Vray, Sketchup
Floor Area: 1820 m²
Site Category: Private Urban Park
Location: Asunción
Structure Composition: Steel Reinforced Concrete, Steel Beams
Façade: Concrete, Glass, Black Aluminum Frames
Materials: Marble and Granite Exterior Cladding, White Marble Floors, Plastered Brick Walls, Drywall Panels

Background

In South America, the arts and exhibition spaces have been historically dominated by white European men and their descendants. Even in present times, art galleries are off-limits spaces for underrepresented artists such as indigenous people, women, and people from the LGBTQ+ community, who are often discriminated against for their race, sexuality, and/or economic status.

The Idea

The proposal envisions the design and creation of an open, safe, and inclusive space where artists from diverse backgrounds and socioeconomic statuses can showcase their creativity. The Unknown Gallery’s mission is to amplify the voices of artists often silenced and relegated to exclusion and anonymity.

Design Concept

The design follows a sober and minimalist style where the architecture aims at highlighting the artworks on display. Bright spaces composed of straight lines and simple geometric shapes are proposed.

Neutral colors and noble natural materials were selected to achieve architectural plasticity that conveys order, durability, and elegance. The spatial organization is quite simple. The exhibition rooms follow a horizontal linear organization, where freedom is absolute.

The glass openings not only allow visual connection but also provide protection, as it creates a space where artists feel free but safe. White color takes over the interiors with the goal of symbolizing a blank canvas at the disposal of the artists.

Program & Operation

Art Gallery: the exhibition gallery consists of five interconnected sequential rooms, three of which are exclusive for graphic arts and two which are for mixed use, to display sculptures and temporary installations.

Theater: a space for theatrical expression, singing, and dancing is located in the basement of the building to provide good acoustics, lighting, and extra privacy for the performers.
