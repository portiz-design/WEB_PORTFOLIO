THE UNKNOWN, GALLERY

Exhibition Complex for Indigenous & Underrepresented Artists

Architect: Pedro Ortiz
Project Category: Academic, “Architectural Studio VIII” course
Institution: Universidad Americana
Media: AutoCAD, Vray, Sketchup
Floor Area: 1820 m²
Site Category: Private Urban Park
Location: Asunción
Structure Composition: Steel Reinforced Concrete, Steel Beams
Façade: Concrete, Glass, Black Aluminum Frames
Materials: Marble and Granite Exterior Cladding, White Marble Floors, Plastered Brick Walls, Drywall Panels

Background

Across South America, artistic institutions and exhibition spaces have long been shaped by a narrow cultural establishment. As a result, many artists from underrepresented communities have remained excluded from institutional platforms, their voices often unseen or unheard.

The Idea

The Unknown Gallery proposes a space where those voices can emerge. The project envisions an open and inclusive cultural institution where artists from diverse backgrounds and socioeconomic contexts can present their work freely.

Rather than reinforcing traditional hierarchies within the art world, the gallery seeks to create a place where creativity, identity, and expression can exist without barriers.

Design Concept

The architecture adopts a sober and minimalist language so that the artworks themselves remain the central focus. Bright spaces defined by clear geometries and a restrained material palette establish an atmosphere of calm and clarity.

The spatial organization is intentionally simple. Exhibition rooms unfold horizontally in a linear sequence, creating a continuous gallery experience. Supporting programs are vertically organized, allowing the building to maintain a clear and legible structure.

Program & Operation

Theater: A performance space for theater, singing, and dance located in the basement, providing controlled acoustics, lighting, and privacy for performers.

Offices: Offices supporting the management and daily operations of the gallery and its programs.

Art Gallery: Five interconnected exhibition rooms form a sequential gallery. Three are dedicated to graphic arts, while two operate as flexible spaces for sculptures and temporary installations.

Restaurant: The restaurant offers a platform for chefs from underrepresented communities, creating space for culinary traditions that are often overlooked. Through weekly rotating events organized by the gallery, these traditions are shared, preserved, and celebrated.

Rooftop Garden: A rooftop garden of medicinal plants & wildflowers honors Paraguay’s indigenous botanical traditions. The space also functions as an open-air sculpture terrace.

Symbolic Design Elements

White interiors evoke the notion of a blank canvas—an architectural surface ready to receive the voices and creativity of those who occupy it.

A reflecting pool surrounding the entrance and exhibition halls captures the image of the building, its visitors, and the surrounding landscape, reinforcing the idea of art as a reflection of human experience.

Large glass openings create fluid connections between interior spaces and the surrounding environment, reinforcing the idea of openness while still offering a protected space for artistic expression.
